The Anti VT-Shaped Student

That sure looks like a VT-Shaped student, though.

I don't have a problem with the VT-Shaped Student initiative. I understand the purpose of recruiting and building the university. But the VT-Shaped Student initiative isn't built for me and doesn't include me. Here are some reasons why.

How do I fit here?

Not to toot my own horn, but I'm a pretty well rounded individual.

  • I'm committed to learning within my major. My grades are good. I interact with my professors and try to make my own work reflect well on my program.
  • I have enjoyed courses outside of my major and related them to my own studies, personal and work lives.
  • Building this website is an example of how I'm using technology to enhance my learning. English majors didn't do things like this in the past!
  • I even did an English department internship with Montgomery County Public Schools.

Sounds like I can check off my VT-Shaped checklist. But here's the thing, and this is why I consider myself an Anti VT-Shaped Student: anything I've done at Virginia Tech to build myself as a good student and person has been intuitive or brought on naturally from my course of study. I didn't even know about the VT-Shaped Student until I started this class two weeks ago! My interests and developments aren't based in the person the University wants me to be, but in the person I want to build myself in to.

This is a VT ENGL 3844 production.
