What makes you so qualified, Michael Carter?

In order to establish a few last words on the VT-Shaped Student, I need to take a good look at myself and my interests to decide what makes me qualified for analysis.

Let's Look at Wine

Wine (as a general topic) is the focus of my Podcast project. It is also one of my most “T-Shaped” interests. I love both drinking it, and selling it. Sure, you might be thinking what any normal adult would think when hearing that a college student is interested in wine. It's just a tool for inebriation, right? Wrong! Wine is an industry, science, academic field, exercise in honing senses, and many more great things. I hate to drink it in excess, and it is best appreciated in small increments.

Virginia Tech offers a class on wine with up to 1000 students per semester offered. Why offer a class on wine? Because it is “T-Shaped.” I go in to more detail here in my podcast, but simply put, this Virginia Tech elective course provided me with an introduction to a field that I could potentially have a career in. Not just a job, but a career. Isn't that the point of college?

So even though my degree isn't in Viticulture or Viniculture, my education has lead me to a new interest and a potential career. This is what the “VT-Shaped Student” is all about, but I didn't know that until about a month ago, when I started ENGL 3844.

Am I Write?

I am happy to write that working through the English program at Virginia Tech has made me a better writer than ever before. Again, college doing it's job. But if you tell someone that you are a “writer” you are bound to get some funny looks and doubtful expectations. I've found that we, as English students, need to broaden our definition of “writing.”

Writing today exists in more diverse fashions than ever before, many of which we covered in this course. Podcasting is a type of writing. Webbuilding is writing. Anytime you create or post content to social media or digital means it is recorded, and therefore in a sense “written.”

So when I say that today I am a better “writer” than I have ever been, I mean that in a number of ways. When I'm working through a course or communicating with other students or professors, I feel like I'm practicing writing. Whether this involves pen, paper, typewriter, computer screen, or none of the above, writing is all around us.

So I've made it through college and I am a better writer. What do I do with that now? These are the things I've thought about in forming my podcast and final project.

Maybe I'm Not the Super Student I Thought I was...

Like I stated in the introduction, the first half of this website was particularly focused on my detachment from the VT-Shaped Student Concept. But I've done some thinking recently...and I'm soon to be a graduate. It's nearly time to start from square one, with less help than ever before. On my own in the so-called “real world.” Good for me for making it through school and recieving grades, but what next?

It's going to be time to apply my learning! I feel that this get's lost on my generation! Yes, many of us came to this university to recieve a diploma with our name on it, but applying our learning towards our “next steps” is just as important. It can be easy to get lost out there.

So I think that this is an important area where being “VT-Shaped” could come in handy. What does being a well-rounded student mean for graduates?
